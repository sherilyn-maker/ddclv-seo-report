Schema & On-Page Fixes to Improve CTR

💡
Schema = Visual Upgrades in Google Search Results

Schema markup (JSON-LD) tells Google to show richer information next to your listing — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, price ranges. These make your listing physically larger and more trustworthy than plain competitor listings at the same position. Add these to your WordPress pages using the Yoast SEO Schema tab or a custom HTML block in the page footer. The code goes between <script type="application/ld+json"> tags.

🚨 Critical — Fix This Week

🔴
1. Meta descriptions missing on all ~35 service-area pages

Every /service-areas/ page — Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas, Paradise, Green Valley, Summerlin and all their sub-service pages — has no meta description. Google generates its own snippet, usually pulling poorly chosen text. This is the single most widespread CTR suppressor on the site.

Fix: Write city+service specific meta descriptions. Pattern: "[Service] in [City], NV starting at $49. Same-day service, licensed & insured. Call 24/7." ~2 min per page.

🔴
2. "Product" schema applied sitewide to service, about, FAQ, blog, and contact pages

The entire site uses "@type": "Product" in JSON-LD on pages where it makes no sense — service pages, about, contact, snaking, rooter, sewer, blog. This misrepresents every page to Google, blocks rich result eligibility, and signals poor structured data quality.

Fix: Replace with correct types — Service on service pages, LocalBusiness on homepage, AboutPage on about, FAQPage on FAQ, Blog on blog index, ContactPage on contact.

🔴
3. FAQ page has no FAQPage schema — biggest single missed rich result

/about-us/faq/ has the "Product" schema but no FAQPage JSON-LD, despite literally being the FAQ page. FAQ dropdowns in SERPs increase CTR 15–25% and can appear even for positions 5–15. This fix takes 10 minutes.

Fix: Add FAQPage JSON-LD to /about-us/faq/. Template is in the Schema tab.

🔴
4. Keyword cannibalization: /services/plumbing/ vs /services/emergency-services/

Both pages target "24-Hour Emergency Plumbing Las Vegas" as their primary keyword. Title tags are nearly identical. They compete against each other and split the authority that should concentrate on one page.

Fix: Pick one page as the canonical emergency page (recommend /services/emergency-services/). Rewrite /services/plumbing/ to target general plumbing repair, or consolidate both into one page with a 301 redirect.

🔴
5. Broken internal links — Summerlin pages link to Las Vegas sewer URL

At least 3 Summerlin pages (/summerlin-nv/, /summerlin-nv/hydro-jetting/, /summerlin-nv/sewer-pipe-cleaning/) have sewer pipe cleaning internal links pointing to the Las Vegas city URL. This passes PageRank to the wrong page and sends users to the wrong city.

Fix: Find and replace the Las Vegas sewer URL with the correct Summerlin URL in the Summerlin page templates. 15 minutes.

🔴
6. Geo-mismatch: /service-areas/paradise-nv/hydro-jetting/ mentions "Henderson" in body copy

The Paradise hydro jetting page body text refers to "Henderson" — a copy-paste error from the Henderson template. Google parses city mentions in body text for local relevance signals. This actively hurts the page's Paradise rankings.

Fix: Search the Paradise hydro jetting page for "Henderson" and replace all instances with "Paradise." 5 minutes.

🔴
7. Title typo: /service-areas/paradise-nv/trenchless-pipe-lining/
